Thrissur Pooram

Where It All Began

In the heart of Kerala, there lived a visionary ruler — Sakthan Thampuran — whose dream was to unite the people of Thrissur in a festival like no other. In the late 18th century, when smaller temples were barred from participating in the Arattupuzha Pooram, he brought them together to form a celebration of their own: Thrissur Pooram.

What followed was not just an act of inclusion, but the birth of a grand tradition. Elephants adorned in gold, rhythmic percussion echoing through the sky, and thousands gathered with devotion — Thrissur Pooram became the heartbeat of a people.

More than two centuries later, the festival still rises each year like a living legend. It is where gods meet under umbrellas of silk, where culture dances through the streets, and where even the sky seems to tremble with joy.
